Where dental follow-ups usually leak.

Most missed opportunities do not happen because the team does not care. They happen because every follow-up competes with calls, check-ins, insurance questions, payments, and patients standing at the desk.

Missed calls

Patients call when the team is busy. If the office does not respond quickly, they may book somewhere else.

Overdue recall

Recall lists exist, but ownership, timing, and message quality often break down before patients schedule.

Unscheduled treatment

Treatment is discussed, but the follow-up path is unclear after the patient leaves.

What it handles — and what it does not.

The point is operational recovery, not clinical decision-making.

  • Handles missed-call text-back, recall outreach, treatment follow-up, reactivation, and reschedule routing.
  • Uses human review before outreach when patient context or message sensitivity matters.
  • Does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace clinical judgment.
  • Does not require your team to change the entire front-desk process on day one.
