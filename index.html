A simple recovery layer for everyday dental operations.

The workflow is based on the three screens most offices need: a clean follow-up list, a human review queue, and a conversation layer that creates the next step.

1

Follow-up list

Organize recall due patients, dormant patients, missed follow-ups, treatment-related touchpoints, and reschedule intent by priority.

2

Human review

Show the patient context, follow-up reason, suggested message, intent level, and next action before anything goes out.

3

Conversation layer

Send approved reminders, understand replies, continue scheduling conversations, and create clean handoff tasks for staff when needed.

What it helps recover

  • ✓Recall and hygiene follow-ups that sit in the PMS too long.
  • ✓Patients who asked to reschedule but never got a clean path.
  • ✓After-hours questions that should become a next-day task.
  • ✓Treatment follow-ups that depend on someone remembering to call.
  • ✓Dormant patients who need a simple, contextual reactivation message.
Hi, I missed your call. Is this about my cleaning?
Yes. The office wanted to help you schedule your hygiene visit. Would morning or afternoon usually work better?
Afternoon, but I need to check insurance first.
Got it. I’ll ask the team to confirm your insurance details before scheduling.
Clean handoff created Intent: recall scheduling. Barrier: insurance question. Next action: front desk review.

What it does

  • ✓Drafts patient outreach based on approved visit context.
  • ✓Flags urgent, unclear, or sensitive replies for staff.
  • ✓Keeps the team in control before messages are sent.
  • ✓Turns messy follow-up work into a repeatable workflow.

What it does not do

  • ×It does not diagnose patients.
  • ×It does not replace clinical judgment.
  • ×It does not blindly message patients without staff control.
  • ×It does not replace the front desk. It recovers work that gets lost.
